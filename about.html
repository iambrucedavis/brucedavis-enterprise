About

AI Product Engineer. Designer who codes. Engineer who designs. Fifteen years on systems you had to trust — money, identity, health records — and on the design details that made them feel trustworthy. Now pointing both halves at AI products that earn the second visit.

The Arc

The work started at Wells Fargo on payment infrastructure — moving money between banks on rails like SWIFT and Fedwire, zero room for error. Then HIPAA-regulated IAM for 16,000+ users at Veradigm. Security engineering across 60+ Starbucks domains. Cloud Solutions Architect at Microsoft, automating identity governance for Entra ID and Non-Human Identity.

Two through-lines ran underneath all of it. The engineering one — identity, authorization, audit, trust: who's allowed to do what, and how do you prove it. And the design one — the question nobody puts on a resume: how does this actually feel to use, and why do users trust the screen in front of them? Every project was secretly a UX project too.

Neither problem shrunk when AI arrived. They got bigger. Non-Human Identity governance — the work at Microsoft — is the same shape as the problem now defining how enterprises deploy AI agents. The interface trust problem is the same one, scaled up by a model that can talk back.

The career didn't pivot. It compounded.

Today I build AI products end to end. Engineering side: Claude workflows, MCP servers, document pipelines. Design side: AI-native interfaces, motion systems, interaction patterns. Both halves, same job. The proof is in Work and the Lab.

How I Work

On a product team, I do three jobs without the handoff gaps that usually break the work. Design side: UX-first thinking, motion as a UX decision, creative direction and aesthetic taste applied to every surface. Engineering side: React, TypeScript, AI-native tooling (Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot) as everyday muscle, not experiments. Delivery side: PMP-certified, fifteen years coordinating regulated rollouts — the rare AI engineer who can also run the program around the launch. I prototype to think. I ship to learn. The parts most people skip — empty states, error states, the moment something breaks — are where products earn trust.

Currently

Charlotte, NC. Lately I'm into card games, whodunit mysteries, and interactive new media — the kind of work where engagement is the design problem. The Lab is the portfolio version of that obsession.

What I'm Looking For

I want to build AI products people actually return to — engagement systems, generative UI, the interaction layer where AI meets a real human workflow. Best fits: AI Product Engineer roles, Applied AI Engineer roles at product-led teams, hybrid pods that want one builder who can design and engineer the loop. Regulated industries (healthcare, finance, fintech) are home turf. Creative tools, film, games, narrative, and new-media teams are where I'd most love to point this stack.

Full-time, contract, and project work — all welcome. Remote-friendly.
